Tyre Care

Caring for your tyres are paramount to your safety and tyre longevity.
The following will ensure that these are maintained:




Wheel Alignment
When your wheels are not aligned your vehicle will tend to veer to the left or right when the steering wheel is pointed straight ahead. Safety and tyre wear is thereby compromised due to the scrubbing effect this has on your tyres. Periodic wheel alignment should be done to prevent uneven and excessive wear and ensure driving safety is maintained.


Tyre Pressure
Incorrectly inflated tyres will compromise safety, tyre wear and fuel consumption. Over inflated tyres will lead to greater wear in the centre of the tyre and can eventually result in a blowout. Under inflated tyres will experience increased wear and fuel consumption due to an increase in tyre temperature from greater surface friction with the road. Periodically check your tyre pressures and ensure that these are consistent with vehicle specifications.


Tyre Rotation
Tyres wear at different rates from back to front and from left to right of the vehicle. It is therefore important that tyres are rotated periodically between the various positions to prevent uneven wear. Where tyre sizes differ from back to front, only left to right and right to left rotation is possible.


Visual Check
Visual checks can go a long way in ensuring maximum safety is maintained and your tyres go the distance. If something looks peculiar, visit your local fitment centre and have it checked.

If all else fails, simply visit your local fitment centre and request a check. This is usually done free of charge. Your tyres are the only thing keeping your vehicle on the road and occupants safe, take care of them.
